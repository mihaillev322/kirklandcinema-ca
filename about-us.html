Accessibility and inclusive viewing

We believe that cinema should be accessible to everyone, and we're committed to providing guidance that supports inclusive moviegoing experiences. Throughout our content, we address considerations for visitors with diverse needs, including those who benefit from captions, audio description, sensory-friendly environments, and mobility accommodations.

When planning your visit, consider the following general accessibility factors:

  • Captioning and subtitles: Many theatres offer open caption screenings or closed caption devices. Check the showtime listings for accessibility indicators, and don't hesitate to call ahead to confirm availability.
  • Audio description: Some cinemas provide audio description tracks for visitors who are blind or have low vision. These narrated tracks describe visual elements during dialogue pauses.
  • Sensory-friendly screenings: These special showings typically feature reduced sound levels, increased lighting, and a more relaxed environment where movement and noise are welcomed. They're designed for individuals with autism, sensory processing differences, or anyone who prefers a less intense atmosphere.
  • Mobility and seating: Accessible seating areas with space for wheelchairs and mobility devices are standard in modern cinemas. Companion seating is usually available adjacent to accessible spaces. Arrive early to ensure you can be accommodated comfortably.
  • Service animals: Service animals are generally welcome in Canadian cinemas in accordance with accessibility legislation.
  • Concession considerations: If you have food allergies or scent sensitivities, it's worth asking concession staff about ingredients and preparation methods. Some visitors bring their own snacks due to dietary restrictions—check the theatre's outside food policy in advance.

For comprehensive information about accessibility rights and standards in Canada, visit the Government of Canada's Accessible Canada initiative, which outlines federal accessibility legislation and resources.

We continuously work to ensure that our own website meets accessibility standards, including semantic HTML, keyboard navigation support, and screen reader compatibility. If you encounter any barriers while using Kirkland Cinema, please let us know so we can improve.
